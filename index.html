Synopsis

For the past thirty years Aden has devoted his life to fight aside England as the head knight during the Edwardian war. Aden creates a reputation for himself as the most vicious warrior on the battlefield only to protect a deep secret as hidden as possible. When the secret is exposed he is forced to strike a deal with a stranger to save his family. His soul is condemned to eternity as he is obligated to stand as the leader of a war unknown to mankind. When his family is taken from him, Aden is ashamed and defrauded by the deal he once believed was a gift. He sets upon a journey to rid of the curse which takes him across the world to Lithium City in the twentieth century. After centuries of searching, Aden finally finds a young man by the name of Josh Krestin. During a freak accident Josh becomes the new vessel for the curse that centuries ago was cast upon Aden. As Josh's life crumbles, he discovers abilities he believed impossible for any human to possess and a dark world he never knew that coexisted with the daily world he lives in. As a new life unfolds for him his old life starts to fade from existence. He soon encounters that the future he once desired and dreamed of, may no longer be his choice to make.

About Mark

My name is Mark Cindell, I’m a writer, debut author, gamer, blogger, foodie, etc. The list goes on and on. I found my passion and creativity when I was a kid after reading “Charlie and the Chocolate Factory” by Roald Dahl. I’m not lying when I say that by the age of twelve I had read this book at least minimum 10 times. The world that Mr. Dahl created, so colorful and adventurous, each character having their own distinctive personality. I felt as if I were sitting right there beside Charlie while he watched as each brat fall prey to their selfish greedy needs. I loved that book and still re-visit whenever I need to find inspiration. After this book, I started off writing short stories for school and some just for myself. Somehow as I grew, the ideas just got shoved aside and I stopped writing. One day at work, I don’t even remember how one of my many story ideas came up in a conversation, and a fellow co-worker said something I’d never forget: “If you have an idea, write it, not everybody can create worlds and characters, so if you can, why let it waste.” I followed those words which soon became the passion that pushed me to publish my debut novel: In Between

Where were you born?

I was born in Chicago Illinoise, close to humble park? it was a dangerouse yet very amazing place to grow up. A lot of gangs, yet I loved the school I attended, von humboldt school, I have very fond memories just like I have a few horrible ones. Just like anything in life, it had it's Pros & Cons

What inspired you to become a writer?

Due to the circumstances where I grew up in Chicago. Usually after school me and my brothers were on lockdown because of the violence just outside our doors. my alternatives to entertainment, were video games, TV and books. So I took turns with each. This is how I came across Roald Dahl's: Charlie & The Chocolate Factory . I couldnt get enough of this book, it was so colorful and vivid. The detail and level of imagination that was used to write that book was on a level of it's own. It's what sparked my interest towards writing. I wanted to reach that level of creativity in my own stories. It's the one book that every now and then I'll sit down and re-read, just to remind myself of why I started writing.
